Phishing Email Detection & Awareness System 
Tools Used
Email Analysis & Investigation Tools:
PhishTank:
Used to collect publicly available phishing URLs and phishing email examples for analysis.
OWASP Foundation:
Used as a reference for phishing awareness concepts and cybersecurity best practices.
Kaggle:
Used to obtain phishing email datasets and sample phishing content.
Google Message Header Analyzer:
Used to understand email header structures and inspect sender-related information.
MXToolbox Email Header Analyzer:
Used for analyzing email routing details and verifying sender authenticity.
Browser Tools / URL Inspection:
Used to safely inspect suspicious links, domains, and phishing URLs without interacting with malicious content.

Analysis Approach
The following methodology was used during the phishing email analysis:
1. Collection of Phishing Samples:
Public phishing email samples and phishing URLs were collected from educational and security-focused sources such as PhishTank, OWASP, and Kaggle.
2. Email Content Analysis:
Each phishing email was reviewed to identify:
Fake sender domains
Generic greetings
Urgent or fear-based language
Suspicious links
Credential harvesting attempts
Social engineering techniques
3. Link & Domain Inspection:
Suspicious URLs and sender domains were inspected for:
Misspelled domains
Fake login pages
Free hosting services
Impersonation attempts
Potentially malicious URLs were safely written using [.] notation to prevent accidental access.
4. Risk Classification:
Each email sample was classified according to its threat level:
Safe
Suspicious
Phishing (High Risk)

5. Security Awareness Assessment:
The phishing techniques used in the emails were analyzed to understand how attackers manipulate users through urgency, fear, trust, and impersonation.

6. Prevention & Awareness Recommendations:
Security recommendations and employee awareness guidelines were prepared to help users identify and avoid phishing attacks.
